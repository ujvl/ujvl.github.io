Overview. My research is motivated by the primary interest of improving the functioning of democratic organizations and institutions, to foster a more just and equitable collective well-being. To this end, I explore how tools from CS, economics and their intersection---such as algorithmic game theory, social choice theory, cryptography and TEEs---can be leveraged to redistribute power and cultivate trust within society. I have also lately been grappling with the question of how to ground this work more formally in (virtue) ethics.

I'm fortunate to be advised by Dawn Song, and am affiliated with RDI & IC3. I'm physically based in NY at Cornell Tech, where I collaborate closely with Ari Juels and others.

Collaboration. Please reach out by email if you're a researcher or practitioner working in relevant areas! I'm particularly interested in hearing from those with expertise in disciplines other than my own (philosophy, political science, economics, sociology, psychology, other social sciences, humanities etc). Happy to talk, collaborate and/or extend resources from my network in support of aligned causes. Be advised: I suffer from a tendency to overcommit and spread myself thin (working on this in therapy).

Past work. I've spent several years meandering through various fields in search of purpose. I spent my undergraduate time at the AMPLab/RISELab building scalable distributed systems grounded in theoretical foundations, with a focus on applications in network systems and machine learning. During my PhD, I pivoted towards security, privacy and applied cryptography. While on leave, I developed an interest in everything else.

Future work. CS x ethics education, incentives in academia, market fundamentalism: I have questions and thoughts about all of these.
