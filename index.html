Overview

I study friction between humans and algorithmic systems, broadly. I'm interested in settings that involve forms of cooperation or competition between agents, e.g. social media platforms, conversational AI assistants and decentralized protocols. Some relevant keywords: decision theory, causal reasoning, algorithmic game theory, social choice, ethics, tech law.

I'm fortunate to be advised by Dawn Song, and am affiliated with RDI & IC3. Previously, my work has also been supported by the Ethereum Foundation. I'm physically based in NY at Cornell Tech, where I collaborate with Ari Juels and others.

Background. I started my PhD in 2022, focusing primarily on security and privacy. I have been on leave working on a startup since 2023, but will be returning in 2026. Earlier in my career, I designed and built distributed systems for machine learning applications at the AMPLab/RISELab.
