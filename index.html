Overview

I study the role of technology and computation in society. I'm currently exploring how tools from particular areas in CS and economics—such as algorithmic game theory, social choice theory, cryptography, and secure hardware can be practically leveraged to cultivate trust, redistribute power and improve the functioning of democratically governed organizations/institutions. I'm also interested in drawing connections to work in ethics, political theory and feminist STS.

I'm fortunate to be advised by Dawn Song, and am affiliated with RDI & IC3. In the past, my work has also been funded by the Ethereum Foundation. I'm physically based in NY at Cornell Tech, where I collaborate closely with Ari Juels and others.

Collaboration. Please reach out by email if you're a researcher or practitioner working in relevant areas! I'm particularly interested in hearing from those with expertise in (1) the social sciences and humanities, and/or (2) governance in practice (at nonprofits, cooperatives, unions, DAOs etc). Happy to talk, collaborate and/or extend resources from my network in support of aligned causes.

Background. Prior to my shift into security and privacy, I worked on building distributed systems for machine learning applications at the AMPLab/RISELab. Since then, my last two years have focused on studying and designing blockchain-based decentralized protocols (both in research and at my startup). Most recently, I've been thinking about the role of secure computation in online deliberation and governance.

Outside work. avid reader, boxer (cal boxing '18), photog. warriors basketball fan. housing/transit policy enthusiast. I also occassionally help some friends run a local Indian food popup—you can help by following @dononyc!
