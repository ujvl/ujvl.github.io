I’m a recent graduate from UC Berkeley and am currently a Software Engineer at Dropbox. I spent my undergraduate time at the AMPLab and RISELab building scalable systems grounded in strong theoretical foundations. My research interests lie broadly in systems and networks.

Research Projects

Ray (ongoing)
Ray is a flexible, high-performance distributed execution framework.

Confluo (ongoing)
Confluo is a system for real-time monitoring and analysis of data, that supports:
  • high-throughput concurrent writes of millions of data points from multiple data streams;
  • online queries at millisecond timescale; and
  • ad-hoc queries using minimal CPU resources.
Load balancing in Replicated Caches (ongoing)
We present (reproducible) experimental evidence that load balancing of requests in replicated caches may lead to multiple orders of magnitude reduction in overall system throughput.

Succinct
Succinct is a data store that enables efficient queries directly on a compressed representation of the input data. Succinct uses a compression technique that achieves compression close to that of gzip and yet allows random access into the input data.
